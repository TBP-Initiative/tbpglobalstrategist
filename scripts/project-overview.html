1. What is TBP Global Strategist?

TBP Global Strategist is a digital platform designed to manage and support the TBP (The Borderless Project) Strategist Pathway. It serves as the central hub for strategists at every stage of their journey — from initial candidate onboarding through to paid advisory and implementation roles.

The platform operates within the Neo-Polar Neutrality Global System (NPNGS) — a multi-trillion dollar framework designed to move beyond gridlocked multilateralism and enable nations, businesses, and citizens to engage through neutral trade corridors, interoperable infrastructure, and shared digital/physical protocols while preserving sovereign identity.

The platform enables:

  • Onboarding & Progression — Structured 6-stage pathway with clear tasks and deliverables at each level
  • Work Area Management — Assignment of strategists to 11 specialized work areas
  • Project Collaboration — Browse, contribute to, and manage TBP projects across 6 core categories
  • Reporting & Feedback — Submit reports, reflections, and task feedback in standard document formats
  • Messaging & Communication — Direct messaging and conversation tools between strategists
  • Administration — Admin tools for user management, stage progression, role assignment, and oversight

4. Progression Stages

Stage 1 — Global Strategist Candidate
Onboarding & Orientation. Complete forms, sign agreements, review TBP materials, and identify where your expertise fits.
Stage 2 — Global Strategist
Exploratory & Contribution-Based. Attach to work areas, prepare research notes, project summaries, and sector opportunity maps.
Stage 3 — Strategic Contributor
Active Contribution. Support defined workstreams with project mapping, technical research, design development, and economic analysis.
Stage 4 — Project-Aligned Strategist
Project-Specific Work. Attached to a specific TBP project or corridor, contributing to brief development, technical notes, and investor-readiness material.
Stage 5 — Sector Lead or Workstream Lead
Leadership & Coordination. Coordinate contributors, set research priorities, review outputs, and report progress to TBP leadership.
Stage 6 — Paid Project Adviser
Paid Implementation Roles. Eligible for paid advisory, research, coordination, technical, or implementation roles on active funded projects.

8. Getting Started

Prerequisites

  • You must be invited or approved to join the TBP Global Strategist pathway
  • A valid email address
  • Internet access to reach the platform

Platform Access

The platform is accessible via web browser at https://tbpglobalstrategist.vercel.app. Supported browsers include Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions).

9. Registration & Onboarding

Step 1: Create Your Account

  1. Navigate to the platform login page
  2. Click Register to create a new account
  3. Provide your full name, email address, and create a secure password
  4. Submit the registration form

Step 2: Complete Your Profile

  1. After logging in, navigate to your profile settings
  2. Add your professional details including title, bio, and years of experience
  3. Select your sector and areas of expertise
  4. Add your location (city and country)
  5. Optionally add links to your LinkedIn profile and personal website
  6. Save your profile

Step 3: Review Onboarding Materials

As a Stage 1 Candidate, your focus should be on learning and orientation. Key materials to review:

  • TBP pathway documentation
  • TBP Self-Onboarding Pack
  • NPNGS materials
  • TBP's physical project portfolio
  • Understanding of trust, capital and corridor participation

Step 4: Submit Your Onboarding Reflection

Using the Submit Report / Task Feedback tool on your dashboard, submit a one-page reflection covering your understanding of TBP, aligned sector, project interests, expertise, and questions. Accepted formats: PDF, DOCX, PPTX, XLSX, CSV, TXT, RTF, ODT, ODS.
